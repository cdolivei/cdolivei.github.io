About a year ago I finished building my soundsystem.
It's a fairly small system, using two reflex subwoofers and a vented mid-top box, but it was... multiple years in the making.

Typically one gives a name to their soundsystem.
For example, the soundsystem I got inspiration from was called Dub Connection here in Toronto, but has sine changed to higher heights soundsystem after the crew disbanded.

For the longest time, I actually had a name chosen for my soundsystem.
It was meant to be called the "wadadada soundsystem" after a verse in Dadawah's track, seventy-two nations, in his Peace And Love album.

> In my land of Ethopia, wadadasow means love man

But even before my soundsystem was finished, I found out that this name was already taken.
Searching now, I cannot find who/what took it, but it was relagated to a name of last resort.
I _could_ take the same name, but I was constantly looking for a new and better name.
And I've been struggling for the past... 9 months to find a suitable name.

Today I think I found it.

> As strangers we came, slave trade was begin <br>
> And ever since we don't even know our name

— From Count Ossie and the Mystic Revelations, 400 years

Groundation is a fantasic album.
And that particular song hits different, and that _particular_ verse hits the hardest for me.

Randomly, today, I realized having _no_ name is perhaps the right name for my soundsystem.
It is a reminder that as a consequence of the trans-atlantic slave trade, people had to abandon their african names and take on new names.

They had lost their names.

So today, and in the future, my soundsysyem will just have no name.
It's symbolic of course.
But it makes for a good story.
